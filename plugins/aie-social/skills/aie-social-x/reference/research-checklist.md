# Research Checklist (X)

Research first. The bar: **every factual claim in the finished post/thread maps to a real, citable source.** X rewards specificity — a real number, a real result, a real timeframe — so the research directly feeds what makes the post work.

## Step 1 — Define the one idea

- What single idea does this post/thread make? One sentence. A thread is that one idea developed, not a grab-bag.
- Who's the reader and what do they get? Specific and personal beats abstract.
- What's the stance? On X, a clear point of view drives replies; fence-sitting gets scrolled.

## Step 2 — Gather (web search + fetch)

Run multiple searches. Hunt for:

- **Hard numbers** — stats, results, benchmarks, dollar amounts, dates. X hooks live on specificity.
- **Primary sources** — original study, release, docs, dataset. Fetch the source, not a summary.
- **Recency** — prefer the last 12-18 months for fast-moving topics; record every date.
- **Counter-takes** — the strongest opposing view. Anticipating it makes the thread credible and reply-worthy.
- **Concrete examples** — named, specific, real. One good example beats three abstractions.

If a source document was provided, verify its claims and find the sharpest current angle.

## Step 3 — Build the source log

```
Claim → Source (URL) → Date → Primary or secondary?
```

- Every factual claim gets a row. No row, no claim.
- Conflicting sources: prefer primary/authoritative, or hedge honestly in the copy.
- Unsourceable claim → cut it, or label it clearly as opinion ("my take:", "IMO").
- Never fabricate a stat, study, quote, or attribution.

## Step 4 — Decide what survives

- Pick the 1-3 facts that best serve the one idea. In a thread, one strong fact or example per body post is plenty.
- Lead with the most surprising/credible fact; it usually becomes the hook.

## Failure modes to avoid

- **Trimming input instead of researching** — the value is fresh facts and a sharp angle.
- **Vague attribution** — "studies show," "experts agree." Name it or cut it.
- **Stale numbers** presented as current. Check dates.
- **Fact-dumping** — a thread that's five stats in a row reads like a spreadsheet. One idea (and ideally one fact) per post.
