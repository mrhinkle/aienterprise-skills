# Hook Library (LinkedIn)

The first ~210 characters are all that shows before "…see more." They decide whether the post gets read at all. Posts opening with a question, a bold statement, or a specific observation meaningfully outperform generic openers.

**Rules for every hook:**
- Under ~210 characters. Ideally the first line stands alone and creates an open loop.
- Specific beats clever. A real number, a real timeframe, a real stakes.
- One line, then a line break. White space pulls the eye down.
- The hook must be honest — if it promises something the body doesn't deliver, trust dies and the algorithm's authenticity checks punish it.

Always draft **3 hooks** from different patterns below, lead with the strongest, keep the rest as alternates.

## Patterns (with fill-in templates)

**1. Contrarian / myth-buster**
Challenge a widely held belief.
> "Most [audience] think [common belief]. After [experience/data], I'm convinced the opposite."
> "[Popular tactic] is quietly costing you [outcome]. Here's what I'd do instead."

**2. Specific number / stat**
Lead with a concrete, sourced figure.
> "[X]% of [group] [surprising fact]. That number changed how I think about [topic]."
> "We cut [metric] by [X]% in [timeframe]. One change did most of the work."

**3. Surprising observation**
State something the reader hasn't framed this way.
> "The best [role]s I know all do one boring thing: [thing]."
> "[Common tool/trend] didn't change [X]. It changed [unexpected Y]."

**4. Question that opens a loop**
A real question the reader wants answered.
> "Why do [group] keep [doing X] when [Y] is obviously better?"
> "What if the reason [problem] is [non-obvious cause]?"

**5. Story / moment-in-time**
Drop the reader into a specific scene.
> "[Timeframe] ago, [specific situation]. Here's what it taught me about [topic]."
> "A [person] asked me [question] last week. I didn't have a good answer — until now."

**6. Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS) opener**
Name the pain, twist it, promise relief.
> "[Painful problem]. And it's getting worse because [reason]. There's a fix most people skip:"

**7. Before-After-Bridge (BAB) opener**
Show the gap, then the path.
> "For years, [old painful state]. Now [better state]. The bridge was [one idea]."

**8. Confession / lesson learned**
Vulnerability that earns trust (only if true).
> "I got [topic] wrong for [timeframe]. Here's the mistake — so you can skip it."

**9. Direct address / callout**
Speak to a specific reader and their situation.
> "If you're a [role] staring at [problem], this one's for you."

**10. Listicle / promise of specifics**
Signal a concrete, skimmable payoff.
> "[N] [things] that [specific outcome] — no fluff:"

## Anti-patterns (do not open with these)

- "In today's fast-paced world…" and any wind-up throat-clearing.
- "I'm excited/humbled to announce…" (unless it genuinely is an announcement, and even then, add tension).
- Generic questions with obvious answers ("Do you want to grow your business?").
- Emoji-stuffed openers or ALL CAPS shouting.
- Clickbait the body can't cash ("This ONE trick will change everything").
