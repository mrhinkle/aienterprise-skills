## 2. The post (delivered)

```
Microsoft Japan tried a 4-day week and productivity went up 40%.

Not down. Up.

The detail everyone skips: they didn't just remove a day. They capped meetings at 30 minutes and moved most updates to async.

The UK ran the bigger experiment — 61 companies, six months. When it ended, roughly 92% of them kept the schedule.

Here's what those trials actually changed:

• Meetings got shorter, fewer, and optional
• Status updates moved to writing
• "Busy" stopped counting as output

The companies that failed at 4-day weeks tried to compress 5 days of old habits into 4. The ones that succeeded redesigned the week first.

If you're curious whether it would work for your team, start with the meeting audit — you can run that without changing anyone's hours.

Have you seen a shorter-week experiment up close? What actually broke, and what didn't?
```
