# Hook Library (X)

On X you have roughly 1.5 seconds. The hook (the single post, or the first post of a thread) must stand alone and earn engagement by itself — before anyone has committed to reading further. In a thread, if the hook doesn't land, nothing else gets seen.

**Rules for every hook:**
- Stands alone. It should be a good post even if the thread stopped there.
- Specific and personal. A real number, a real timeframe, a real mistake, a real result.
- Fits in one post (default 280 chars). Front-load the payoff; don't bury the point.
- Honest. If the hook over-promises and the body underdelivers, replies turn on you and reach dies.
- No hashtag in the hook, no external link in the hook, no ALL CAPS.

Draft **3 hooks** from different patterns, lead with the strongest, keep the rest as alternates.

## Patterns (with fill-in templates)

**1. Bold claim**
> "[Confident, specific assertion]. Here's why."
> "[Common practice] is dead. What's replacing it:"

**2. Specific promise / listicle**
> "[N] [things] that [specific outcome]:"
> "[N] free tools that replaced [$X/mo] of software:"

**3. Contrarian take**
> "Most [topic] advice is wrong. Here's what actually works in 2026:"
> "Everyone says [X]. After [experience/data], I disagree:"

**4. Curiosity gap**
> "I analyzed [N] [things]. The pattern was obvious once I saw it:"
> "There's one reason [outcome] that nobody talks about:"

**5. Personal result / specificity**
> "I [result] in [timeframe]. Here's exactly how:"
> "I spent [$X] learning [thing] so you don't have to:"

**6. Step-by-step process**
> "How to [desirable outcome] (step by step):"

**7. Story / moment-in-time**
> "[Timeframe] ago, [specific situation]. What it taught me:"

**8. Direct address / callout**
> "If you're [role] struggling with [problem], you're probably making these mistakes:"

**9. Credibility hook**
> "After [N] [projects/years/clients], I've noticed a pattern:"

**10. Surprising stat**
> "[X]% of [group] [surprising fact]. Let that sink in:"

## Thread body patterns

- Each post = one complete idea (tip, step, data point, example). Readable in isolation.
- Number them (1/, 2/…) or let X's native threading carry order — but keep each post self-contained.
- Vary post shapes: a one-liner, then a short example, then a data point. Monotony kills scroll-through.
- Last post: a real CTA — "Follow for more on [topic]" or "Repost if this helped." Put any link here or in a reply.

## Anti-patterns (do not open with these)

- Wind-up throat-clearing ("A thread 🧵 on why...") with no actual hook in the first line.
- Engagement-farming ("Like if you agree, RT if you disagree") — suppressed in 2026.
- 3+ hashtags (triggers spam filtering) or a hashtag jammed into the hook.
- External link in the hook post (suppresses reach).
- Vague, obvious questions ("Want to grow on X?").
- Over-promise the body can't cash.
