# User Journey Questions by Page Type

For each major page, judge it against what a real visitor is trying to do.

## Homepage
- Is the value proposition clear within about 5 seconds?
- Is the primary CTA obvious?
- Are there intuitive entry points to the key sections?
- Does a first-time visitor understand what this site is for?

## About / Services / Product
- Does it answer "Who are you?" and "Why you?"
- Are credentials, team, or proof of trust visible?
- Is there a clear next step (CTA) from the page?
- Is jargon explained or avoided?

## Contact
- Is the form simple and unintimidating?
- Are multiple contact options offered (form, email, phone)?
- Is an expected response time stated?
- Is the form above the fold on both desktop and mobile?
- Do tap-to-call / tap-to-email work on mobile?

## Blog / Resources / Docs
- Is search present and working?
- Do categories and tags aid discovery?
- Are related items suggested at the end?
- Is there enough content to justify the section?
- Are long articles broken up with headings and lists?

## Cross-cutting checks on every page
- Working escape route (logo → home).
- Consistent header/footer/nav.
- Clear, single primary action.
- No dead ends or orphaned states.
