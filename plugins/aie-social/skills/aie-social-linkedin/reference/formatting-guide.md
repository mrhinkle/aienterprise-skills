# Formatting & Algorithm Guide (LinkedIn, 2026)

## Length

- **Character limit:** 3,000.
- **Sweet spot:** ~1,300-1,900 characters for most posts. The 1,300-2,500 band shows the highest median engagement in 2026 data. Long enough for a full arc (hook → context → insight → takeaway → close), short enough to read in 60-90 seconds.
- Shorter (under ~600) works for a single sharp observation. Longer (toward 3,000) works for a genuine story or framework — but earn every line.

## Structure (the arc)

1. **Hook** — first 1-2 lines, under ~210 characters (see hook-library.md).
2. **Context / setup** — 1-3 short lines that frame the stakes or the problem.
3. **Body** — the one idea, developed. Use a mini-framework, a short list, or a story. One idea only.
4. **Concrete takeaway** — the specific, usable thing the reader leaves with.
5. **Soft close** — a real question or invitation to add experience. No engagement-bait.

The **3-2-1 rule** is a useful default: 3 candidate hooks (pick 1), 2 concrete takeaways/insights, 1 clear close.

## Formatting for skimmability

- **One to two sentences per paragraph.** Hard line breaks between them. Walls of text get scrolled past.
- **White space is a feature.** A blank line between thoughts increases dwell time.
- Use a short bulleted or numbered list when you have parallel points — but keep bullets to a phrase or one line each.
- Avoid heavy special-character formatting (fake bold via unicode, arrow salad). It reads as spam and hurts accessibility.
- Emoji: minimal or off by default. If used, one to signal structure, not decoration. Honor any config that bans them.

## 2026 algorithm rules that change how you write

- **Zero-click / no outbound links in the body.** LinkedIn suppresses reach for posts that send readers off-platform. Deliver ~80% of the value in the post itself. Put any link in the **first comment** or the Featured section, and tell the reader it's there.
- **Saves > likes.** A save signals lasting reference value and earns extended distribution over days. Write posts worth saving: frameworks, checklists, specific how-tos, reference lists.
- **Dwell time matters.** Posts that hold attention 30-60 seconds get maximum distribution. That's what the white space, the arc, and the "…see more" hook are buying.
- **Engagement-bait is penalized.** The 2026 authenticity update suppresses posts that explicitly beg for interaction ("comment YES," "tag 3 people," "like if you agree"). Ask a real question instead, or just make a point worth responding to.
- **Reply fast.** Responding to early comments (first ~30 min) meaningfully lifts reach. Note this for the human — it's a posting behavior, not a copy change.
- **Consistency of topic.** The algorithm learns your lane over weeks. Not this post's job, but worth telling the human if they're building a presence.

## Hashtags

- 0-3, topical, at the very end. They aid a little categorization; more than 3 looks spammy. Not a growth lever in 2026 — semantic understanding does the categorizing.

## Formats worth suggesting (when the topic fits)

- **Document / carousel post** — top performer; each swipe adds dwell time. Suggest when the idea is a multi-step framework or a "N lessons" list.
- **Text + single image** — solid default.
- **Native video** — strong reach; suggest when the human has footage.

Recommend the format, but this skill's deliverable is the copy.
